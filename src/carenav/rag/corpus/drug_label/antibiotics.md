# Antibiotics

## Indications and usage

Antibiotics are medicines that fight infections caused by bacteria. They are used to treat
conditions such as strep throat, urinary tract infections, skin infections, and some
respiratory and intestinal infections. Common antibiotics include amoxicillin, penicillin,
cephalexin, ciprofloxacin, doxycycline, nitrofurantoin, and sulfamethoxazole/trimethoprim.
Antibiotics do not work against viral illnesses such as colds, the flu, or most sore
throats, so they are prescribed only when a bacterial infection is suspected or confirmed.

## Dosage and administration

Antibiotics are given in several forms, including pills, capsules, and liquids taken by
mouth, creams and ointments applied to the skin, and injections or IV infusions for more
serious infections. Take the medicine exactly as prescribed and at the recommended times,
and follow any instructions about taking it with or without food. Finish the full course
even if you feel better, because stopping too soon may let some bacteria survive and cause
the infection to return. Do not save antibiotics, share them, or use a prescription that
was meant for someone else.

## Warnings and precautions

Overuse and misuse of antibiotics can lead to antibiotic resistance, in which bacteria
change and no longer respond to the medicine, so antibiotics should be taken only when
needed. Tell your prescriber and pharmacist about any drug allergies, especially to
penicillin or other antibiotics, and about any past reactions. Antibiotics can interact
with other medicines, vitamins, and supplements, so share a full list of what you take.
People who are pregnant or breastfeeding should confirm that a chosen antibiotic is safe.

## Adverse reactions

Common side effects include rash, nausea, diarrhea, and yeast infections. Get medical care
right away for signs of a life-threatening allergic reaction such as trouble breathing,
swelling of the face or throat, or hives, and for severe or watery diarrhea that can signal
a Clostridioides difficile (C. difficile) infection affecting the colon. Report any side
effect that is severe, does not go away, or worries you to your prescriber.
