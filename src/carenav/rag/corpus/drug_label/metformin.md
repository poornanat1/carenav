# Metformin Hydrochloride

## Indications and usage

Metformin hydrochloride is indicated as an adjunct to diet and exercise to improve
glycemic control in adults and children with type 2 diabetes mellitus. It is often the
first oral medicine prescribed for type 2 diabetes. It is not used to treat type 1
diabetes.

## Dosage and administration

Metformin is taken by mouth with meals to reduce stomach upset. The dose is started low
and increased gradually based on glucose control and tolerability. Doses and schedules
are individualized by the prescriber. Do not change your dose or stop taking metformin
without talking to your clinician. Swallow extended-release tablets whole; do not crush
or chew them.

## Warnings and precautions

Metformin carries a boxed warning for lactic acidosis, a rare but serious buildup of
lactic acid in the blood. Risk is higher with kidney impairment, dehydration, excessive
alcohol use, and certain imaging procedures that use contrast dye. Stop metformin and
seek care for symptoms such as unusual muscle pain, trouble breathing, unusual
sleepiness, or severe stomach pain. Kidney function is checked before and during
treatment.

## Adverse reactions

The most common side effects are gastrointestinal: diarrhea, nausea, upset stomach, and
gas, which often improve over time or when the medicine is taken with food. Long-term
use can lower vitamin B12 levels.
