# Chemotherapy Medications

## Indications and usage

Chemotherapy medicines are used to treat cancer by killing cancer cells or stopping them
from growing and dividing. They may be used to cure cancer, to lower the chance that it
comes back, to slow its growth, or to ease symptoms by shrinking tumors that cause pain or
other problems. Cytotoxic agents such as cisplatin, paclitaxel, doxorubicin, and etoposide
and the targeted agent bevacizumab treat many cancer types, while hormonal agents such as
tamoxifen and letrozole are used for hormone-sensitive breast cancer.

## Dosage and administration

These medicines are given in different ways depending on the drug and the cancer, including
by mouth, by injection, or by intravenous (IV) infusion through a vein or catheter. Agents
such as cisplatin, paclitaxel, doxorubicin, etoposide, and bevacizumab are most often given
by infusion, while tamoxifen and letrozole are usually taken by mouth. Treatment is given on
a set schedule in cycles, with rest periods that let healthy cells recover, and the dose is
set by the prescriber. Do not change the dose or skip doses without talking to your care
team.

## Warnings and precautions

Chemotherapy can harm healthy cells as well as cancer cells, so it must be given exactly as
directed and monitored with regular blood tests and checkups. Many of these drugs can lower
blood counts and weaken the immune system, and some, such as doxorubicin, can affect the
heart, so report fever, chills, or other symptoms promptly. These medicines may interact
with other drugs, vitamins, and herbal products, so tell every provider and pharmacist about
everything you take. People who are pregnant, planning pregnancy, or breastfeeding should
discuss risks, as many chemotherapy and hormonal agents can harm a developing baby.

## Adverse reactions

Common side effects include nausea, vomiting, fatigue, hair loss, mouth sores, and an
increased risk of infection; hormonal agents such as tamoxifen and letrozole may also cause
hot flashes and joint pain. Get medical care right away for signs of serious problems such
as fever or other signs of infection, unusual bleeding or bruising, chest pain or shortness
of breath, severe diarrhea or vomiting, or numbness and tingling in the hands or feet.
