# Seizure and Anti-Anxiety Medications

## Indications and usage

These medicines are used to control seizures and to calm abnormal electrical activity in the
brain. Carbamazepine is used to control certain types of seizures, to treat the nerve pain of
trigeminal neuralgia, and to treat manic or mixed episodes in bipolar I disorder.
Benzodiazepines such as clonazepam, diazepam, and midazolam are used to control seizures and
to treat anxiety, and they are also used to relax muscles, treat alcohol withdrawal, or
provide sedation before procedures. Clonazepam may be used to prevent and control certain
seizures and to treat panic attacks.

## Dosage and administration

These medicines come in several forms, including tablets, extended-release tablets, capsules,
liquid, and injections given by a clinician. Carbamazepine is usually taken by mouth with
meals, started at a low dose and increased gradually, and extended-release tablets must be
swallowed whole and not crushed or chewed. Take the exact dose prescribed at the same times
each day, and do not stop suddenly, because stopping a seizure or benzodiazepine medicine
abruptly can cause seizures or withdrawal. Blood levels and bloodwork may be checked
regularly while you take these medicines.

## Warnings and precautions

Tell your clinician about liver disease, kidney disease, breathing or lung problems, glaucoma,
mental health conditions, pregnancy or plans to become pregnant, and all other medicines,
supplements, and alcohol you use. These medicines can cause drowsiness and dizziness, so do
not drive or operate machinery until you know how they affect you, and avoid alcohol and other
sedating drugs because the combination can dangerously slow breathing. Carbamazepine can cause
serious skin reactions such as Stevens-Johnson syndrome (with higher risk in people of Asian
ancestry), can lower blood cell counts, may make hormonal birth control less effective, and
interacts with many drugs and with grapefruit juice. Benzodiazepines can be habit-forming and
may cause dependence, and all of these medicines may increase the risk of suicidal thoughts or
mood changes, so report any new depression, anxiety, or behavior changes promptly.

## Adverse reactions

Common side effects may include drowsiness, dizziness, unsteadiness or coordination problems,
dry mouth, constipation, and tiredness. Get medical care right away for a rash with fever or
blistering, yellowing of the skin or eyes, easy bruising or signs of infection, vision
changes, a fast or irregular heartbeat, or new or worsening thoughts of self-harm. Seek
emergency help for slowed or stopped breathing, severe drowsiness or confusion, severe
swelling, or an allergic reaction such as swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat or
trouble breathing.
