# Albuterol Inhalation

## Indications and usage

Albuterol is a quick-relief bronchodilator used to treat and prevent breathing problems
caused by asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), including difficulty
breathing, wheezing, shortness of breath, coughing, and chest tightness. It can also be
used to prevent breathing trouble brought on by exercise. It works by relaxing and opening
the airways to make breathing easier.

## Dosage and administration

For sudden symptoms, the inhaler is typically used every 4 to 6 hours as needed. To prevent
exercise-induced symptoms, it is usually used 15 to 30 minutes before activity. Follow the
manufacturer's instructions for priming and using the inhaler, and do not use it more often
than directed. Needing quick-relief medicine more and more often can be a sign that asthma
is not well controlled—contact your clinician.

## Warnings and precautions

Tell your clinician about heart disease, irregular heartbeat, high blood pressure, an
overactive thyroid, diabetes, or seizures, and about other medicines you take, especially
beta blockers. Rarely, albuterol can cause wheezing or trouble breathing right after it is
inhaled; if this happens, get medical help right away.

## Adverse reactions

Common side effects include tremor or shakiness, nervousness, headache, throat irritation,
and a fast heartbeat. Get medical attention right away for chest pain, a rapid or irregular
heartbeat, worsening breathing trouble, or an allergic reaction such as rash, hives, or
swelling of the face, lips, or throat.
