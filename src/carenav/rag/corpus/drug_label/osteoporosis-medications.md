# Osteoporosis Medications (Bisphosphonates)

## Indications and usage

Bisphosphonates are used to treat and prevent osteoporosis, a condition in which the bones
become thin and weak and break more easily, and they help lower the risk of fractures.
Common bisphosphonates include alendronate, and they work by slowing the breakdown of bone
and increasing bone density. Some of these medicines are also used to treat osteoporosis
caused by long-term steroid use and Paget's disease of bone. They are often prescribed along
with adequate calcium and vitamin D.

## Dosage and administration

Oral bisphosphonates such as alendronate are taken by mouth on an empty stomach, first thing
in the morning, and some forms are taken once daily while others are taken once weekly. Take
the dose just after getting out of bed with a full glass (6 to 8 ounces) of plain water
only, and do not eat, drink, or take other medicines, supplements, or antacids for at least
30 minutes afterward. Swallow tablets whole and remain sitting or standing upright for at
least 30 minutes after the dose to protect the esophagus. Keep taking the medicine as
prescribed even if you feel well, since osteoporosis usually has no symptoms.

## Warnings and precautions

Tell your clinician about kidney disease, low blood calcium, trouble swallowing, stomach or
esophagus problems, dental problems or planned dental surgery, and pregnancy or plans to
become pregnant. These medicines are not recommended for people who cannot sit or stand
upright for at least 30 minutes, and they can irritate or damage the esophagus if not taken
correctly. Calcium and vitamin D supplements, antacids, and other medicines can interfere
with absorption and must be separated from the dose, and using NSAIDs may increase the risk
of stomach irritation. Rare but serious risks include osteonecrosis (bone loss) of the jaw,
especially around dental work, and unusual fractures of the thigh bone.

## Adverse reactions

Common side effects may include nausea, stomach pain, heartburn, constipation, diarrhea, gas,
bloating, headache, dizziness, and muscle or joint aches. Get medical care right away for
severe heartburn, chest pain, pain or difficulty when swallowing, new or worsening bone,
muscle, or joint pain, jaw pain or a tooth or jaw that does not heal, new hip or thigh pain,
or eye pain or vision changes. Seek emergency help for signs of an allergic reaction such as
swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat, or trouble breathing.
