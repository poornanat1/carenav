# Lisinopril

## Indications and usage

Lisinopril is an angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitor. It is indicated for the
treatment of high blood pressure (hypertension) in adults and children, for heart
failure, and to improve survival after a heart attack. It may be used alone or with
other medicines.

## Dosage and administration

Lisinopril is taken by mouth once daily, with or without food. The starting dose and
any adjustments are set by the prescriber based on blood pressure response and kidney
function. Take it at about the same time each day. Do not stop taking it without
talking to your clinician, even if you feel well, because blood pressure may rise again.

## Warnings and precautions

Lisinopril carries a boxed warning: when used during pregnancy it can cause injury or
death to the developing fetus, so it should be stopped as soon as pregnancy is detected.
A serious allergic reaction called angioedema — swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or
throat with trouble breathing — is a medical emergency; seek care immediately. The
medicine can raise blood potassium and affect kidney function, which are monitored with
blood tests.

## Adverse reactions

Common side effects include a dry cough, dizziness (especially when standing up),
headache, and tiredness. Dizziness can be worse with the first dose or after a dose
increase. This label text is general drug information and is not a substitute for advice
from the prescriber or pharmacist.
