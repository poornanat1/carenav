# Pain Relievers (NSAIDs and Acetaminophen)

## Indications and usage

Over-the-counter pain relievers ease mild to moderate pain such as headaches, sore muscles,
backache, arthritis, menstrual cramps, and the aches that come with colds and fever.
Acetaminophen relieves pain and lowers fever and is often chosen for general aches.
Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) such as ibuprofen and naproxen relieve pain,
reduce fever, and also lower inflammation, so they often work well for pain that involves
swelling, such as arthritis or menstrual cramps.

## Dosage and administration

These medicines are taken by mouth and are sold without a prescription in tablets, capsules,
and liquids. Follow the directions on the package label and do not take more than the
recommended amount or use it longer than directed. Without first talking to a healthcare
provider, do not use these products for more than ten days for pain, or more than five days
for pain in a child. Because acetaminophen is found in many cold, flu, and combination
products, check every label to avoid taking more than one product that contains it.

## Warnings and precautions

Taking more than the recommended amount of acetaminophen, including doubling up on products
that contain it, can cause serious liver damage. Taking more NSAID than directed can cause
nausea, stomach pain, and ulcers, and long-term use may affect the kidneys. Talk to a
provider or pharmacist before use if you drink alcohol regularly, take a blood thinner, have
stomach, liver, kidney, or heart disease, or are pregnant or breastfeeding, and tell them
about all other medicines you take.

## Adverse reactions

Common side effects of NSAIDs include nausea, upset stomach, and heartburn, while
acetaminophen is usually well tolerated at recommended doses. Stop the medicine and get
medical care right away for signs of stomach bleeding such as black or bloody stools or
vomiting blood, for an allergic reaction such as rash, swelling, or trouble breathing, or if
you suspect that too much was taken.
