# Antidepressants

## Indications and usage

Antidepressants are used to treat depression and a range of related conditions, and the
selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI) class includes medicines such as fluoxetine,
sertraline, citalopram, and escitalopram. Depending on the specific drug, they may also be
used for obsessive-compulsive disorder, panic attacks, certain anxiety disorders, some
eating disorders, and premenstrual dysphoric disorder. These medicines work by increasing
the amount of serotonin, a natural substance in the brain that helps regulate mood. Some
antidepressants are combined with other medicines to treat depression in bipolar disorder.

## Dosage and administration

Antidepressants such as fluoxetine are usually taken by mouth, often once daily in the
morning, and are available as capsules, tablets, delayed-release forms, or liquid. Take the
exact dose your clinician prescribes at about the same time each day, and keep taking the
medicine even if you do not feel better right away, since full effects can take four to five
weeks. Do not stop the medicine suddenly without medical guidance, because stopping abruptly
can cause withdrawal symptoms. Have follow-up visits as directed so your response and dose
can be reviewed.

## Warnings and precautions

Antidepressants may increase the risk of suicidal thoughts or actions, especially in people
under 24 and during the first weeks of treatment or after a dose change, so report new or
worsening depression, thoughts of self-harm, agitation, panic, or sleep problems right away.
Tell your clinician about all medicines and supplements you take, and do not use these
medicines with MAO inhibitors, pimozide, thioridazine, or St. John's wort. Use caution with
NSAIDs and aspirin, which can raise the risk of bleeding, and discuss pregnancy or
breastfeeding with your clinician. These medicines may cause drowsiness, so use care when
driving or operating machinery until you know how they affect you.

## Adverse reactions

Common side effects may include nervousness, trouble sleeping, nausea, dry mouth, weakness,
sweating, and sexual problems. Get medical care right away for signs of an allergic
reaction, chest pain, irregular heartbeat, seizures, abnormal or unusual bleeding,
hallucinations, or any thoughts of harming yourself. Seek emergency help for serotonin
syndrome, which can cause agitation, confusion, fast heartbeat, high fever, or muscle
stiffness.
