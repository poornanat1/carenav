# Other Medications (Fluoride and Supplies)

## Indications and usage

This group covers miscellaneous medications and supplies that do not fit into a single therapy
class, such as fluoride dental gel and saline (saltwater) solution. Topical fluoride gel,
foam, or rinse is used to strengthen tooth enamel and help prevent cavities, especially in
people at higher risk of tooth decay or with dry mouth. Saline solution is used to rinse,
moisten, or irrigate areas such as the nose, eyes, wounds, or contact lenses, and is also used
to dilute or flush other medicines and intravenous lines. These products treat or prevent
local problems rather than a specific systemic disease.

## Dosage and administration

Follow the product label or your provider's or dentist's directions, because forms, strengths,
and schedules differ widely across these items. Fluoride dental gel is usually brushed or
applied to the teeth, often at bedtime; after applying, spit out the excess and avoid eating,
drinking, or rinsing for about 30 minutes so the fluoride can work, and do not swallow it.
Saline solution is applied as a rinse, spray, drop, or irrigation to the intended area, and
sterile saline should be used for the eyes, wounds, or any injectable or flushing use. Use only
the product and amount directed, and do not reuse single-use containers.

## Warnings and precautions

Keep fluoride products out of the reach of children, since swallowing large amounts can be
harmful, and tell the dentist if a child tends to swallow rather than spit out dental products.
People with mouth sores, recent oral surgery, or trouble swallowing should check before using
fluoride gel, and anyone whose water is already fluoridated should mention this to avoid too
much fluoride. Use the correct type of saline for the task; plain table-salt mixtures or
non-sterile water are not safe for the eyes, wounds, or injection. Tell your provider or
pharmacist about all medicines, supplements, and health conditions, and about pregnancy or
breastfeeding, so the right product and amount can be chosen.

## Adverse reactions

Fluoride gel may cause temporary tooth sensitivity, gum or mouth irritation, an unpleasant
taste, or nausea if swallowed, while saline rinses may cause mild stinging, dryness, or
brief discomfort at the site used. Stop the product and get care for signs of an allergic
reaction such as rash, itching, swelling, or trouble breathing, for severe or worsening pain,
redness, or swelling at the treated area, or for nausea, vomiting, drooling, or stomach pain
after swallowing fluoride. If too much fluoride or another product is swallowed, contact Poison
Control at 1-800-222-1222 or seek emergency care.
