# Atorvastatin

## Indications and usage

Atorvastatin is a statin medicine used to lower cholesterol and reduce the risk of heart
attack and stroke. It works by slowing how much cholesterol the body makes, which helps
prevent buildup in the arteries that can restrict blood flow. It is used together with a
heart-healthy diet.

## Dosage and administration

The tablet is taken by mouth once daily, with or without food, at about the same time each
day. Keep taking it even if you feel well, because high cholesterol usually has no
symptoms. Do not stop taking atorvastatin without talking to your clinician. The dose is
individualized based on cholesterol levels and response.

## Warnings and precautions

Tell your clinician about liver problems, heavy alcohol use, muscle disorders, diabetes,
kidney disease, or pregnancy, and about other medicines you take. Atorvastatin can affect
the liver, so liver function may be monitored. It should not be used during pregnancy.
Report unexplained muscle pain, tenderness, or weakness promptly.

## Adverse reactions

Common, usually mild side effects include diarrhea, heartburn, gas, and joint pain. Get
medical attention right away for muscle pain, tenderness, or weakness; unusual tiredness or
lack of energy; signs of liver problems such as yellowing of the skin or eyes or dark
urine; or an allergic reaction such as rash, hives, or trouble breathing.
