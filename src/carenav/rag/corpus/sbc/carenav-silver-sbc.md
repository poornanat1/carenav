# CareNav Silver — Summary of Benefits and Coverage

This Summary of Benefits and Coverage (SBC) is a synthetic example built for the CareNav
demo, formatted to mirror the federal SBC template. The numbers correspond to the
CareNav Silver plan in the demo dataset.

## Overall deductible

The overall plan deductible is $2,500 per year for an individual. Generally you must pay
all of the costs from providers up to the deductible amount before this plan begins to
pay. The deductible applies before coinsurance for most services.

## Out-of-pocket limit

The out-of-pocket limit for this plan is $6,000 per year for an individual. The
out-of-pocket limit is the most you could pay in a year for covered services. After you
reach this limit, the plan pays 100% of the costs of covered benefits for the rest of
the plan year. Premiums and out-of-network charges do not count toward the limit.

## Common medical services

For a primary care visit to treat an injury or illness, you pay a copay and the
deductible does not apply. For a specialist visit, a higher copay applies. For
diagnostic imaging such as an MRI or CT scan, you pay coinsurance after the deductible,
and prior authorization is required. If you do not get required prior authorization,
benefits may be reduced or denied. Emergency room visits are covered, and the
emergency-room copay is waived if you are admitted to the hospital.

## Prescription drugs

Generic drugs are covered at a low copay. Preferred brand drugs cost more, and
specialty drugs are covered at coinsurance. Some drugs require step therapy or prior
authorization. Covered drugs are listed on the plan formulary, which can change during
the year.

## Excluded and limited services

Services this plan generally does not cover include cosmetic surgery, long-term care,
and routine dental care for adults. Some services such as physical therapy are covered
but limited to a set number of visits per year. Always check the full plan documents,
because this summary does not list every covered and excluded service.
