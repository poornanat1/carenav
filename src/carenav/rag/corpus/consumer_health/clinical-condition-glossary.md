# Clinical Condition Glossary

## Kidney and diabetes terms

Microalbuminuria means a small but abnormal amount of albumin is present in the urine.
Albumin is a protein normally found in blood. Albumin in urine can be an early sign of
kidney damage or kidney disease, especially in people with diabetes or high blood
pressure. It is commonly evaluated with a urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio, also called
ACR or UACR.

Proteinuria means protein is present in the urine. A high amount of protein in urine can
mean the kidneys are leaking protein and may be damaged. Albuminuria is a form of
proteinuria that refers specifically to albumin in the urine.

Diabetic kidney disease means kidney damage related to diabetes. Diabetes and high blood
pressure are common causes of chronic kidney disease, and urine albumin testing can help
find kidney damage early.

## Heart disease terms

Myocardial infarction means heart attack. An acute ST-segment elevation myocardial
infarction, often called STEMI, is a type of heart attack pattern seen on an
electrocardiogram when blood flow to part of the heart muscle is blocked.

Ischemic heart disease means the heart muscle is not getting enough blood and oxygen,
most often because coronary arteries are narrowed by plaque. Coronary artery disease is
the most common form of ischemic heart disease.

Coronary artery bypass grafting, often called CABG or bypass surgery, is an operation
that creates a new route for blood to flow around blocked or narrowed coronary arteries.
"History of coronary artery bypass grafting" means the person previously had that bypass
surgery; it is a past medical-history item rather than a new diagnosis by itself.

## Lipid and metabolic terms

Hypertriglyceridemia means the level of triglycerides, a type of fat in the blood, is too
high. Triglycerides are measured on a lipid panel along with LDL and HDL cholesterol.

Hyperlipidemia means there are high levels of fats, also called lipids, in the blood.
These fats can include cholesterol and triglycerides.

Metabolic syndrome means a cluster of risk factors, such as high blood pressure, high
blood sugar, abnormal cholesterol or triglyceride levels, and excess weight around the
waist, that increase the risk of heart disease and type 2 diabetes.

## Eye and diabetes terms

Diabetic retinopathy means diabetes has damaged blood vessels in the retina, the
light-sensitive tissue at the back of the eye. Nonproliferative diabetic retinopathy is
an earlier stage; proliferative diabetic retinopathy is more advanced and involves new
abnormal blood vessel growth.

Macular edema related to diabetic retinopathy means fluid has built up in the macula, the
part of the retina used for sharp central vision.
