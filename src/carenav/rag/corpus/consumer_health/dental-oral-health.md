# Dental and Oral Health

## What it is

Dental and oral health covers the care of the teeth, gums, and mouth. Keeping the mouth
healthy helps with eating, speaking, and overall well-being. Common conditions in this
group include cavities and tooth decay, gum disease, tooth loss, bad breath, mouth sores,
and teeth grinding.

## Symptoms and signs

Oral health problems can show up as tooth pain or sensitivity, swollen, red, or bleeding
gums, and persistent bad breath. Cavities may cause aches or visible holes in the teeth,
while gum disease can make gums tender or cause them to pull away from the teeth. Some
conditions, such as early decay, may cause no symptoms at first and are found during a
routine exam.

## How it is diagnosed or evaluated

A dentist evaluates oral health through regular dental exams that check the teeth, gums,
and soft tissues of the mouth. Dental x-rays, including panoramic images, can reveal decay
between teeth, bone loss, and problems below the gum line that are not visible by sight.
Cleanings and checkups also give the dental team a chance to spot early problems.

## How it is managed or treated

Good daily habits are the foundation of oral health: brushing twice a day with fluoride
toothpaste and cleaning between the teeth daily with floss or another cleaner. Limiting
sugary foods and drinks and avoiding tobacco lower the risk of decay and gum disease.
Professional care may include cleanings, fillings for cavities, and treatment for gum
disease or other conditions.

## When to seek care

See a dentist for regular checkups and for ongoing tooth pain, bleeding or swollen gums,
loose teeth, mouth sores that do not heal, or persistent bad breath. Seek prompt care for
severe pain, facial swelling, or signs of infection such as fever.
