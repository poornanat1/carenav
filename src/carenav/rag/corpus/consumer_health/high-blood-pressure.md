# High Blood Pressure (Hypertension)

## What is high blood pressure

Blood pressure is the force of blood pushing against the walls of your arteries. High
blood pressure, also called hypertension, means that pressure is consistently too high.
A normal reading is below 120/80 mm Hg. Blood pressure is considered high at 130/80 mm
Hg or above. High blood pressure usually has no symptoms, so the only way to know you
have it is to measure it.

## Why it matters

Over time, high blood pressure damages arteries and forces the heart to work harder. It
is a leading risk factor for heart attack, stroke, heart failure, and kidney disease.
Because it is symptomless, it is sometimes called a "silent" condition. Regular
measurement and follow-up are the way it is caught and controlled.

## Lowering your blood pressure

You can lower blood pressure with lifestyle changes: eat a diet rich in vegetables,
fruits, and whole grains and low in sodium; be physically active most days; reach and
keep a healthy weight; limit alcohol; and avoid tobacco. When lifestyle changes are not
enough, clinicians may prescribe blood-pressure medicines. Take medicines as directed
and keep follow-up appointments to monitor your readings.

## When to get emergency care

A blood-pressure reading of 180/120 mm Hg or higher with symptoms such as chest pain,
shortness of breath, weakness or numbness, difficulty speaking, or vision changes can be
a hypertensive crisis and is a medical emergency. Call emergency services right away.
