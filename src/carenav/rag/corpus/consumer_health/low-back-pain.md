# Low Back Pain

## How common it is

Back pain is one of the most common medical problems, affecting about 8 out of 10 people
at some point in their lives. Pain can range from mild discomfort to severe. Acute back
pain usually lasts a few days to a few weeks, while chronic back pain lasts longer than
three months.

## Recovery and self-care

Most back pain goes away on its own, though it may take a while. Helpful self-care
includes over-the-counter pain relievers and staying gently active. Long bed rest of more
than a day or two can actually make back pain worse, so light activity often supports
healing better than complete rest.

## When imaging is needed

Imaging such as an MRI or X-ray is not needed for most short-term back pain, because the
pain usually improves on its own and imaging does not change early care. A clinician
decides when imaging is appropriate—for example, when pain follows a serious injury,
persists despite treatment, or comes with warning signs—rather than ordering it for every
case. Advanced imaging like an MRI is also where a health plan may require prior
authorization.

## When to seek care

Contact a clinician if back pain is severe, does not improve after a few days, or follows
an injury. Seek urgent care for back pain with fever, loss of bladder or bowel control,
numbness or weakness in the legs, or unexplained weight loss.
