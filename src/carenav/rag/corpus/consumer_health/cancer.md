# Cancer

## What it is

Cancer is a group of more than 100 diseases in which cells in the body grow and divide in
an abnormal, uncontrolled way. New cells form even when the body does not need them, and
old or damaged cells do not die when they should. These extra cells can form a mass called
a tumor; a malignant tumor can invade nearby tissues and spread to other parts of the body
through a process called metastasis.

## Symptoms and signs

Symptoms depend on the type and location of the cancer, and early cancer often causes no
pain. Common warning signs include a new lump or thickening, unusual bleeding or bruising,
a sore that does not heal, and a change in the size or color of a mole. Other signs are
persistent cough or hoarseness, trouble swallowing, unexplained weight loss, and lasting
fatigue or changes in bowel or bladder habits.

## How it is diagnosed or evaluated

There is no single test for cancer, so diagnosis usually combines several steps. A clinician
reviews personal and family medical history and performs a physical exam, and may order
screening tests such as mammograms or colonoscopies. Blood tests and imaging like X-rays,
CT, or MRI can show suspicious areas, and a biopsy that examines a tissue sample is often
used to confirm whether cancer is present and what type it is.

## How it is managed or treated

Treatment depends on the type of cancer, its stage, and the person's overall health, and
often combines more than one approach. Common treatments include surgery to remove tumors,
radiation therapy, chemotherapy, immunotherapy, hormone therapy, and stem cell or bone
marrow transplantation. Care plans also address symptom relief and support, and may aim to
cure the cancer, control its growth, or ease symptoms when a cure is not possible.

## When to seek care

See a health care provider if you notice a lump, unusual bleeding, a sore that will not
heal, or other symptoms that last for several weeks or keep getting worse. Regular
recommended screening can help find some cancers early, when treatment tends to work best.
