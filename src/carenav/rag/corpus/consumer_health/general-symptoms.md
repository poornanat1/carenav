# Common Symptoms (Fever, Pain, Cough, Fatigue)

## What it is

A symptom is a sign that something may be wrong in the body, and it is not a disease on its
own. This group covers some of the most common symptoms people notice: fever, pain, cough,
and fatigue. A fever is a body temperature raised above the normal range of about 98.6°F
(37°C), pain is a nervous system signal of possible harm, a cough is a reflex that clears
the throat and airways, and fatigue is a feeling of weariness or lack of energy.

## Symptoms and signs

These symptoms can appear alone or together and often point to an underlying infection,
injury, or chronic condition. Fever frequently accompanies infections as the body fights
them. Pain may be acute, coming on suddenly and warning of injury, or chronic, lasting
beyond three months. Coughs may be acute and clear within a few weeks, or chronic and
persistent, while fatigue often builds over weeks and can interfere with daily activities.

## How it is diagnosed or evaluated

Evaluation usually starts with a history of the symptom and a physical exam. Temperature is
measured with a thermometer (oral, rectal, or under the arm), and pain is described by
location, quality, severity, and timing. A clinician may ask about sleep, mood, appetite,
and activity for fatigue, and may use blood tests, imaging, sputum cultures, or other tests
to find the underlying cause when it is not clear.

## How it is managed or treated

Treatment focuses on relieving the symptom and addressing its cause. Drinking enough fluids
helps with fever, cough, and overall recovery, and over-the-counter medicines such as
acetaminophen or ibuprofen can ease fever or pain. Pain may also respond to physical
therapy, exercise, and stress reduction, while fatigue often improves with better sleep,
regular activity, and healthy habits. Aspirin should not be given to children, and cough
medicine should not be given to children under four.

## When to seek care

Seek prompt care for a very high or persistent fever, severe or unexplained pain, a cough
that brings up blood or lasts more than a few weeks, or fatigue that does not improve with
rest over several weeks. Call emergency services for trouble breathing, chest pain, fainting,
or any symptom that feels life-threatening.
