# Obesity

## What it is

Obesity is a condition in which a person carries excess body fat that can harm their
health. It is most often described using body mass index (BMI), a measure that compares
weight to height; in adults a BMI of 30 or higher is considered obesity, and 40 or higher
is severe obesity. Obesity generally develops over time when the calories taken in exceed
the calories the body uses, influenced by eating habits, activity, genetics, certain
medicines, and other medical conditions.

## Symptoms and signs

Obesity itself is identified by excess body weight and fat rather than by specific
symptoms, and many people feel well for years. Over time it can contribute to problems
such as joint pain, shortness of breath, fatigue, and trouble sleeping. It also raises the
risk of serious conditions including type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, heart disease,
stroke, sleep apnea, osteoarthritis, and some cancers.

## How it is diagnosed or evaluated

A clinician usually reviews personal and family health history and performs a physical
exam. Body mass index is calculated from height and weight, and waist circumference may be
measured because a larger waistline points to higher health risk. Blood tests and other
checks help assess related conditions, though BMI has limits and can misjudge body fat in
very muscular people or older adults with less muscle.

## How it is managed or treated

Management centers on healthy eating, increased physical activity, and other lifestyle
changes, often with support from counseling or weight-management programs. Losing even 5
to 10 percent of body weight can help prevent or delay obesity-related diseases.
Prescription weight-loss medicines may be added, and weight-loss (bariatric) surgery is an
option for some people with severe obesity or serious related health problems.

## When to seek care

Talk with a health professional about a weight-management plan and about screening for
related conditions such as diabetes, high blood pressure, and high cholesterol. Seek
prompt care for symptoms like chest pain, severe shortness of breath, or signs of these
complications.
