# Dementia and Alzheimer’s Disease

## What it is

Dementia is a loss of mental functions severe enough to interfere with daily life, affecting
memory, thinking, language, judgment, and behavior. It is not a normal part of aging but a
sign of disease in the brain. Alzheimer's disease is the most common form, marked by brain
plaques and tangles that disrupt nerve cell connections. Other types include vascular
dementia, Lewy body dementia, and frontotemporal disorders.

## Symptoms and signs

Early signs often include forgetfulness and trouble with thinking, reasoning, and finding
words. As dementia progresses, people may get lost in familiar places, forget the names of
loved ones, and struggle with tasks they once managed easily. Personality changes, mood
swings, and difficulty controlling emotions are common. Some types also cause problems with
balance, movement, or alertness.

## How it is diagnosed or evaluated

There is no single test for dementia, so a clinician reviews the medical history and performs
physical and neurological exams. Cognitive and memory tests assess thinking, language, and
problem-solving abilities. Blood tests, brain imaging such as CT or MRI, and sometimes
genetic testing help identify the cause and rule out treatable conditions that mimic dementia.

## How it is managed or treated

Most types of dementia have no cure, so care focuses on preserving function and easing
symptoms. Medicines may help some people with memory and thinking or with mood and behavior
changes, though effects vary. Occupational, speech, and other therapies support daily living,
and music or art activities can reduce anxiety. A safe routine, support for caregivers, and
treatment of other health problems all help maintain quality of life.

## When to seek care

Contact a healthcare provider if memory loss begins to affect daily activities or if there is
new confusion, trouble with language, or concerning changes in behavior or personality. Seek
prompt care for a sudden change in alertness or thinking, which can signal another problem.
