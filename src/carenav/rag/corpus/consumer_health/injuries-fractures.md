# Injuries, Fractures and Sprains

## What it is

Injuries are damage to the body caused by accidents, falls, blows, or other external force.
This group includes fractures (broken bones), sprains and strains affecting ligaments,
muscles, and tendons, lacerations or cuts to the skin, and burns. Many of these injuries
result from car accidents, falls, and sports, while overuse and repetitive motion can also
cause fractures and sprains. Conditions such as osteoporosis can make bones break more
easily.

## Symptoms and signs

The presentation depends on the injury but often involves pain, swelling, bruising, and
tenderness at the affected area. A fracture may cause intense pain, visible deformity, and
reduced ability to move or bear weight. Sprains and strains tend to cause pain, swelling,
and limited motion around a joint or muscle, while cuts bleed and burns cause redness,
blistering, or skin damage.

## How it is diagnosed or evaluated

A clinician evaluates an injury with a physical examination of the affected area and a
review of how it happened. Imaging tests such as an x-ray are commonly used to see whether
a bone is broken, and other imaging may be ordered for more complex injuries. Wounds and
burns are assessed by inspecting their depth, size, and extent of tissue damage.

## How it is managed or treated

The most common treatment for a fracture is wearing a cast or splint to keep the bone still
while it heals, and some breaks require surgery with plates, pins, or screws. Sprains and
strains are often managed with rest, ice, compression, and elevation, along with pain
relief and gradual return to activity. Cuts are cleaned and may need closure, and burns are
treated based on their severity.

## When to seek care

Get medical care right away if you think a bone may be broken, or if there is a visible
deformity, severe pain, heavy bleeding, or a deep or large wound or burn. Seek emergency
care for injuries from major trauma or any loss of feeling or movement in the affected area.
