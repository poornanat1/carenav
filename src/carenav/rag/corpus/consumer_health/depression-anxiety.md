# Depression and Anxiety

## What it is

Depression and anxiety are common mental health conditions that affect how you feel, think,
and handle daily life. Depression is a serious mood disorder that causes persistent sadness
and loss of interest, while anxiety involves excessive worry or fear. These conditions can
occur on their own or together and range from mild to severe. They are medical conditions,
not personal weakness, and they are treatable.

## Symptoms and signs

Depression often causes feeling sad or empty, loss of interest in activities, changes in
appetite or sleep, fatigue, hopelessness, irritability, and unexplained physical aches.
Anxiety can bring constant worry, restlessness, trouble concentrating, a racing heart, and
muscle tension. For a depression diagnosis, symptoms usually persist most of the day, nearly
every day, for at least two weeks.

## How it is diagnosed or evaluated

A healthcare provider evaluates these conditions by reviewing your medical and family
history and current symptoms. A physical exam and lab tests may be done to rule out other
causes such as thyroid problems. A mental health evaluation assesses your mood, thoughts,
and behavior to see whether symptoms meet diagnostic criteria.

## How it is managed or treated

Treatment commonly combines psychotherapy, such as talk therapy, with medicines like
antidepressants; finding the right medicine can take time. Light therapy may help seasonal
patterns, and brain stimulation such as ECT or rTMS is used for severe cases that do not
respond to other treatment. Regular activity, sleep, social support, and avoiding alcohol
also support recovery. Early treatment helps most people manage symptoms effectively.

## When to seek care

Contact a healthcare provider if symptoms last more than two weeks or interfere with daily
life. Seek immediate help or call 911, or contact the 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, if
you or someone else has thoughts of suicide or self-harm.
